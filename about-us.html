PROGRAM FOUNDATION

At Inmates For Change, we are "Dedicated To Breaking The Bondage Of Destructive Thinking". We offer services that are essential to the client, and support them with a host of services that will ensure positive results. Understanding the challenges a returning citizen will face upon his/her release, the I.F.C. Organization provides services and resources that Empower and Motivate the individual to take control of his/her destiny! The challenges faced in addition to lack of opportunities and resources inspired me to start the 501(c).(3) Not-For-Profit Organization: Inmates For Change.

THE RETURNING CITIZEN

A returning citizen is commonly referred to as an ex-offender or convicted felon. The terms ex-offender and convicted felon are derogatory and fail to clearly reflect the ambitions and goals of re-entry. A returning citizen is someone who is transitioning into society, as a law abiding, productive, and concerned community member..., returning citizens are unjustly labeled with individuals who are continually living on the outside of the law. As a returning citizen, I found it extremely difficult to secure gainful employment and integrate back into society. Despite having over twenty-seven years of Professional Sales experience, seventeen years of Managerial experience, worked on the same job for 10 years, and operated my own business for 14 years. During this transition, returning citizens look to family, community organizations, and friends for assistance, support, and sometimes..., direction to overcome the obstacles. Although all support and genuine assistance is appreciated and needed, the returning citizen must be encouraged to evaluate, and motivate him/herself to make a successful transition becoming progressive in thought and action; this we believe, will gradually change the negative perception of the returning citizen. There are considerations to take into account, that enable the returning citizen to acclimate into mainstream society. The first is changing the language. The negative connotations associated with such terms as ex-offender and convicted felon add to the social stigma and negative self image associated with a history of incarceration. Next, the returning citizen must embrace education and training, to successfully re-integrate into mainstream society. Dispelling the negative stereotypical mentality, that returning citizens are not properly educated, trained, or rehabilitated..., but are prone to violence.
